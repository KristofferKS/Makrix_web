The Growing Threat Landscape

As IoT adoption accelerates, cybercriminals are increasingly targeting these often overlooked and inadequately secured devices to gain unauthorized network access and launch sophisticated attacks.

Critical IoT Security Challenges

Weak Authentication

Many IoT devices ship with default or hardcoded credentials that are rarely changed, creating easy access points for attackers to exploit and gain unauthorized system access.

Unencrypted Communications

IoT devices frequently transmit sensitive data without proper encryption, allowing attackers to intercept communications and steal valuable information through man-in-the-middle attacks.

Lateral Movement

Once compromised, unsegmented IoT devices provide attackers with pivoting opportunities to move laterally across networks, reaching critical systems and sensitive data stores.

Irregular Updates

Many IoT manufacturers provide limited or inconsistent security updates, leaving devices vulnerable to known exploits and zero-day vulnerabilities for extended periods.

Botnet Recruitment

Unsecured IoT devices are prime targets for botnet operators who harness their collective power to launch devastating DDoS attacks against critical infrastructure and services.

Limited Visibility

Organizations often lack comprehensive visibility into their IoT ecosystem, making it difficult to identify suspicious behavior, detect compromised devices, and respond to threats.
